The race

Spicy rogaining, at night, from a hundred kilometres out.

Inward Bound is ANU's annual navigation-based endurance race. Teams of four are driven to a remote, unknown point in the country around Canberra and dropped after dark. No GPS — just a map, a compass, your legs and each other. First team to the secret Endpoint wins its division. Griffin fields teams across the grades, from the 30-kilometre lower divisions to the brutal 100-kilometre Division 1.

Race night, in four moves

How a race unfolds

01 · The drop

Into the unknown

Blindfolded in the bus so nobody clocks the route. The doors open somewhere in the dark and the clock starts.

02 · The fix

Where are we?

Read the land, find your position on the map, and choose a line to the Endpoint. Getting this wrong costs hours.

03 · The grind

Move as one

Hills, creeks, fences, fatigue. A team only counts when all four cross together, so nobody gets left behind.

04 · Endpoint

Dawn, and a finish line

The secret Endpoint appears out of the morning fog, crowds cheering each team in. Then you do it all again next year.
