The sport

What is Inward Bound?

Inward Bound is an annual navigation-based endurance race run by ANU students in the country around Canberra. It first ran in 1962, when the university was barely a decade old and Bruce Hall was the only residence on campus. The idea was borrowed from World War II survival training designed for downed pilots: disorient a team, leave them somewhere unfamiliar, and see if they can find their way home.

The format has been made far safer over the decades, but the core has never changed. Teams of four are transported — blindfolded, so nobody memorises the way — to a remote drop point after dark. From there it's just a map, a compass, and your own two feet. No GPS, no phones for navigation, no lift from a stranger. The first team to the secret Endpoint wins its division.

(In that very first race, the winning team reportedly flagged down a passing car and were home in minutes. The rules have tightened since.)

Safety comes first

Modern Inward Bound runs to serious safety standards. Teams carry mandatory gear — first-aid supplies, warm layers, and emergency communication devices that reach Race HQ if anything goes wrong. The event was rebuilt around a comprehensive risk-management plan, and that professionalism is a big part of why it's still running six decades on.

The grades

Seven divisions, plus Division X

A "division" is really a distance. The higher the division, the further from the Endpoint you're dropped and the longer you're out there. Griffin puts teams into the grades that suit its runners each year.

DivisionRough drop distanceCharacter
Division 1~100 km · up to 24 hrsThe blue-ribbon grade. Elite endurance and navigation.
Division 2~80 kmA serious overnight effort just below the top.
Division 3~65 kmLong, but reachable for committed runners.
Division 4~55 kmWhere many strong first-timers land.
Division 5~45 kmEndurance without the full-distance grind.
Division 6~35 kmAn achievable, still-demanding introduction.
Division 7~30 kmThe entry grade — your first taste of the dark.
Division X~45–90 kmOpen to alumni, rogainers and the public in teams of two to four.

Distances vary year to year with the course — treat these as a guide, not a promise.

Our hall

Who are Griffin Hall?

Griffin Hall is ANU's non-residential hall — the first of its kind in Australia, founded in 2010. Every other hall is a building you live in. Griffin is for the students who don't: the ones commuting from across Canberra, the mature-age and postgrad crowd, the locals. Townies, proudly.

Without a corridor to bond over, Griffin builds its community through what it does together — arts, interhall sport, and a common room off the campus's edge. Inward Bound is the sharpest expression of that. A Griffin IB squad is hand-picked from every corner of the capital and asked to do something genuinely hard, as one team.

Any ANU student not already in a residential hall or college can be a Griffin — which means anyone reading this could be on next year's start line.

The build-up

How the squad trains

Months of running before race night, plus the skills you can't get from a treadmill. Our programme is tailored to the mix of runners Griffin brings each year.

Long runs

Weekly distance to build the engine, ramping toward the drop you're training for.

Navigation nights

Map, compass and route choice — practised in the dark, because that's when it counts.

Bush sessions

Off-track movement, hills and creek crossings around Canberra's reserves and the Brindabellas.

Team craft

Pacing together, decision-making under fatigue, and looking after the four of you as one unit.
